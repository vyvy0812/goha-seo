# Search Intent Analysis: Nghị định 148 an toàn thực phẩm

> **Keyword**: nghị định 148 an toàn thực phẩm
> **Primary Intent**: Informational (Analysis & Compliance Guide)
> **Micro-Intent**: Know (Understand new decentralized regulations) & Do (Adjust compliance procedures)
> **User Motivation**: Businesses and Quality Assurance (QA) teams need to understand how the *new* Decree 148/2025/NĐ-CP (effective July 2025) changes administrative procedures, specifically the decentralization of state inspection and certification to provincial levels.
> **Recommended Format**: Comprehensive Analysis & Advisory Article (Scientific/Legal Tone).
> **SERP Features**: Featured Snippet (Definition/Scope), Knowledge Panel (Legal Text).

## 7-Type Classification
1.  **Primary**: **Informational** (Users are seeking the content and impact of the new decree).
2.  **Secondary**: **B2B/Commercial** (Implication for businesses: "How does this affect my export license or testing facility designation?").
3.  **Local**: **Low**, but relevant in the context of *provincial* decentralization (Sở Y tế/UBND cấp tỉnh).

## Content Strategy for WIN Flavor
*   **The Problem**: QA/R&D departments are confused about the shift of power from Central (Cục ATTP) to Provincial authorities (Sở Y tế/UBND).
*   **The WIN Flavor Solution**: As an R&D partner, WIN Flavor provides ingredients that already meet the strictest standards, simplifying the "state inspection" process regardless of *level* (provincial or central). We help navigating the technical dossier requirements that local authorities will now enforce.
*   **Key Themes**: Decentralization (Phân cấp), Import Inspection (Kiểm tra nhập khẩu), Export Certification (Cấp giấy chứng nhận xuất khẩu), Testing Designation (Chỉ định kiểm nghiệm).

## User Journey
1.  **Search**: "Nghị định 148 2025 có gì mới", "thủ tục an toàn thực phẩm nghị định 148".
2.  **Read**: Expects a summary of changes, not just the full legal text.
3.  **Action**: Check if their current certificates need update or where to submit new applications (Provincial vs Central).
