# Fact-Check Report: ống chiller là gì

## 1. Technical Claims Validation

### 1.1. Thermal Conductivity (PPR vs. Steel)
- **Claim**: PPR is hundreds of times more insulating than steel.
- **Verification**: 
    - PPR conductivity: ~0.24 W/mK.
    - Steel conductivity: ~45-50 W/mK.
    - **Ratio**: ~1:200. Claim is **VALID**. Added specific ratio to the article for precision.

### 1.2. Insulation Standards
- **Claim**: 19-25mm is standard for 7°C water.
- **Verification**: 
    - **QCVN 09:2013/BXD** and **TCVN 232.2:202x** specify ~16-20mm for standard indoor conditions (26°C/60% RH).
    - However, for high-humidity factory environments in Vietnam (RH > 80%), engineering practice often requires 25mm to reach the dew point safety margin.
    - **Adjustment**: Refined the article to mention both the TCVN baseline and the "Industrial Engineering" safety margin of 25mm for high-humidity zones.

### 1.3. Cycle Time Impact
- **Claim**: Proper cooling reduces cycle time and energy loss.
- **Verification**: Literature confirms that cooling time is the largest component of cycle time (~50-80%). Lower, stable chiller temperatures enabled by high-quality piping directly shorten this phase. Claim is **VALID**.
